Estate planning information checklist

Information to gather for estate documents

Organize key names, assets, and preferences before requesting preparation of wills, powers of attorney, or related documents.

⚖

General information only. This guide is not legal advice and eligibility or requirements may vary.

Identify the people involved

List the full legal names and contact information of beneficiaries, personal representatives, trustees, guardians, health-care agents, and financial agents you have selected.

Prepare a general asset list

Gather general information about real estate, financial accounts, business interests, vehicles, insurance, and significant personal property. Avoid sending complete account numbers by ordinary email.

Write down your instructions

Document your own choices about distributions, decision-makers, guardians, and health-care preferences before the preparation appointment.

Get legal advice when needed

Estate planning can involve tax, creditor, probate, and family-law consequences. LawMart does not advise which plan or document is appropriate; consult an attorney for legal recommendations.

Protect your privacy: Do not send Social Security numbers, full financial account numbers, or other highly sensitive information through ordinary email or a website form.
